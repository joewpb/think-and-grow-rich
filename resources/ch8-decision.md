# Chapter 8: Decision — The Mastery of Procrastination

## Speed of Decision

Hill observed that successful people make decisions *quickly* and change them *slowly*. Unsuccessful people decide slowly and change frequently. The speed of decision correlates directly with outcome amplitude.

## The Cost of Indecision

Indecision is the primary breeding ground for fear and doubt. Every open loop drains attention. Closing a decision — even a wrong one — is faster than the compound interest of delay.

## Engineering Translation

Decision = commit point. Fast commit with slow revert beats slow commit with fast revert. Time in "maybe" is the most expensive state.
