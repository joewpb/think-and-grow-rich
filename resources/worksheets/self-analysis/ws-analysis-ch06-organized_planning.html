SELF-ANALYSIS: ORGANIZED PLANNING

The Crystallization of Desire Into Action · josie.ai Study Club
Self-analysis is the most productive form of discomfort available to you. The questions below are designed to produce one thing: honest data about how the principle of Organized Planning is or isn't currently operating in your life. Comfortable answers are useless. Accurate answers are the entire point.
Five Diagnostic Questions — Answer Every One
Q01. Is your plan written? If not — why not, and when will you write it?
Your answer here — write without filtering.
Q02. List your Master Mind alliance members and what each contributes specifically.
Your answer here — write without filtering.
Q03. What is the last plan that failed? What specifically was wrong with the plan — not with you?
Your answer here — write without filtering.
Q04. What is your revised plan following that failure?
Your answer here — write without filtering.
Q05. Which of Hill's 11 leadership attributes is your biggest current liability? What's one action to address it this week?
Your answer here — write without filtering.

The One Finding That Matters Most

The value of this exercise is entirely determined by the honesty of your answers — not the neatness of them, not the impressiveness of them. A brutally honest answer to an uncomfortable question is worth more than a week of comfortable reading.
