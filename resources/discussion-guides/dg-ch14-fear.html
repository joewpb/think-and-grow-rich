Chapter 14 · How to Outwit the Six Ghosts of Fear · Discussion Guide

HOW TO OUTWIT THE SIX GHOSTS OF FEAR

Principle: The Final Self-Audit · josie.ai Study Club
Hill saves the most uncomfortable chapter for last. The Six Ghosts — poverty, criticism, ill health, loss of love, old age, death — don't announce themselves. They operate beneath awareness, slowly shutting down ambition, initiative, imagination, and persistence until the person accepts their condition as permanent. Most people carry at least two of these without ever naming them.
Discussion Questions · Session Protocol
Q01 · The Three Enemies First
Before the six fears, Hill identifies three that must be cleared first: Indecision, Doubt, and Fear. He says the Sixth Sense cannot function while any of these three are present. Which of these three is most active in your system right now — and where specifically is it showing up?
Q02 · Fear of Poverty
Hill calls this the most destructive fear and the hardest to master. Its symptoms: indifference, indecision, doubt, worry, over-caution, procrastination. Audit yourself against all six symptoms. Which ones are you running right now — and what are they costing you in terms of forward motion?
↳ Be specific. Vague identification produces no change.
Q03 · Fear of Criticism
Hill says most people don't develop original plans or take clear positions because they fear criticism. Where in your life are you moderating your actions, diluting your goals, or staying invisible to avoid someone's criticism?
↳ Name the specific person or group whose opinion you're managing around.
Q04 · The Susceptibility to Negative Influences
Hill identifies susceptibility to outside negative influences as one of the most dangerous traits — because it allows others to think for you. Who in your environment is currently influencing your thinking in a direction that conflicts with your definite want?
Q05 · The Full Self-Analysis
Hill provides a comprehensive self-analysis questionnaire at the end of this chapter. He says most people who read it will avoid completing it. Complete it. Every question. What did you find that you weren't expecting to find?
↳ This is the most important exercise in the book. Do not skip it.
Run the protocol: Pre-load → Signal → Friction → Capture. Every idea your own mind generates during this discussion belongs in your notes — not the book's ideas. Yours. That is the output Hill designed this club to produce.
