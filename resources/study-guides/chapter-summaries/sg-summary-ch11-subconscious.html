THE SUBCONSCIOUS MIND

The Connecting Link · josie.ai Study Club
"The subconscious mind works day and night. Through a method of procedure unknown to man, the subconscious mind draws upon the forces of Infinite Intelligence for the power with which it voluntarily transmutes one's desires into their physical equivalent."
Chapter Overview
The subconscious never sleeps, never evaluates, and never discriminates between instructions given deliberately and those absorbed by environmental default. It records every thought impulse that reaches it — through deliberate input or through unmanaged exposure — and works continuously to translate those impulses into their physical equivalent. Hill identifies 14 emotions (7 positive, 7 negative) that are the primary currency of subconscious programming, and explains why the positive and negative cannot occupy the mind simultaneously.
Key Concepts at a Glance
Continuous Operation
The subconscious works 24 hours a day. This means it is always running a program — the question is whether that program was installed deliberately or by accident.
14 Emotions
Positive: desire, faith, love, sex, enthusiasm, romance, hope. Negative: fear, jealousy, hatred, revenge, greed, superstition, anger. One set or the other dominates at any given moment. They cannot coexist at equal intensity.
Emotion as the Language
The subconscious does not respond to logic or cold reasoning. It responds to emotion. This is why autosuggestion without feeling doesn't work — the message is transmitted in a language the subconscious doesn't recognize.
The Garden Metaphor
If you don't plant deliberately, weeds grow. The subconscious left without deliberate input defaults to the fears and doubts of the surrounding environment. This is not neutral — it is an actively degrading default.
Prayer as Transmission
Hill reframes prayer as a direct transmission to Infinite Intelligence through the subconscious. He says it works only when approached with faith — not fear. Most people, he observes, pray only after everything has failed, which means they approach the subconscious in its most fear-saturated state.
