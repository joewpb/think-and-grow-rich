KEY CONCEPTS: DECISION

"The value of decisions depends upon the courage required to render them."
Five Concepts to Install — Not Just Understand
Concept 01 · Decide Fast, Change Slowly
The pattern Hill documented across thousands of cases. The speed of the initial decision and the resistance to reversing it without strong cause — this is the operational signature of people who achieve their definite want.
Concept 02 · Opinion Contamination
Opinions are 'the cheapest commodities on earth.' Most people delay decisions by polling people who have no stake in the outcome. The more opinions collected, the less decisive the result.
Concept 03 · The Closed Mouth Principle
Hill: tell the world what you intend to do — but first show it. Premature disclosure of plans invites premature opposition and often kills the plan before it has momentum. The Founding Fathers made their decision in secret for months before announcing it.
Concept 04 · Courage as Prerequisite
Hill says the value of a decision depends on the courage required to make it. An easy decision costs nothing. A decision made despite real risk — financial, social, physical — is where the principle becomes a tool.
Concept 05 · Indecision as Active Drain
Indecision is not neutral. It consumes energy, attention, and momentum continuously. Every day a decision is deferred, the cost compounds — not dramatically, but consistently.
The Operational Test
For each concept above: can you explain it in one sentence without referencing the book? Can you name where it is or isn't currently operating in your life? If not — you understand it. You haven't installed it yet. Understanding and installation are different events. Only one produces results.
These five concepts are the chapter's load-bearing walls. Miss any one of them and the structure doesn't hold.
