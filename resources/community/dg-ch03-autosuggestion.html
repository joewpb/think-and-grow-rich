Chapter 03 · Auto-Suggestion · Discussion Guide

AUTO-SUGGESTION

Principle: The Medium for Influencing the Subconscious · josie.ai Study Club
Auto-suggestion is the installation protocol — the method by which you move an idea from your conscious mind into your subconscious, where it begins to operate automatically. Hill's warning: reading the words is not enough. Emotion is the carrier signal. Without it, you're doing data entry into a disconnected system.
Discussion Questions · Session Protocol
Q01 · The Emotion Requirement
Hill says plain, unemotional words do not influence the subconscious. Most people's affirmation practice is emotionally flat. What does it mean to mix genuine feeling with your statement — and what's the difference between faking emotion and generating it?
↳ This distinction determines whether the practice works.
Q02 · The Conscious Mind as Gatekeeper
Hill says you have absolute control over what enters your subconscious through your five senses — but most people don't exercise it. What is currently entering your subconscious through repeated environmental input that you haven't consciously approved?
Q03 · Why Reading the Chapter Aloud Works
Hill instructs you to read this specific chapter aloud every night until convinced. What is the mechanical reason this produces a different result than reading silently once?
↳ Answer this from the principle, not from assumption.
Q04 · The Twice-Daily Statement
You have (or should have) a written statement of your definite want. Are you reading it twice daily with full sensory visualization? If not — what is the real reason? Not the stated reason. The real one.
Q05 · Concentration as the Input Mechanism
Hill says the ability to use autosuggestion depends on your capacity to concentrate on a desire until it becomes a burning obsession. How developed is your concentration capacity, and what is actively degrading it?
↳ This is a practical systems question, not a philosophical one.
