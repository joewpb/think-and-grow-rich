DESIRE STATEMENT: IMAGINATION

The Workshop of the Mind · josie.ai Study Club
A desire statement is not a goal. It is a written commitment that eliminates the alternative. Write it in the first person, with a specific outcome, a specific date, and a specific exchange.
Your Statement — Fill Every Field
My Definite Want
State the raw idea or undervalued asset you are applying imagination to this week.
The Exact Date
By this date I will have achieved the above. No ranges. No approximations.
What I Give In Return
The specific service, product, or value I provide in exchange. Hill says there is no something for nothing.
Why I Will Not Quit
The non-negotiable reason this outcome is not optional. This is what you read when the exit route looks attractive.
Evidence of Desire (Not Wish)
List 3 specific actions from the last 30 days that prove this is a desire — not a preference.
Template — Combine Your Answers Into One Statement
Your Desire Statement · Read Aloud Twice Daily
I possess _______________ and I am applying imagination to build a plan from it. I use my imaginative faculty deliberately, not passively.
Completion Checklist
My want is stated with specificity — no ranges or approximations
My date is exact — a specific day, not a season or quarter
My exchange is named — I know what I'm giving in return
I have written evidence of desire, not just intention
My statement is written on paper and will be read aloud twice today
A desire statement that you wrote but don't read is a document. A desire statement you read twice daily with emotion is an installation. The difference between those two things is the difference between knowing what you want and actually wiring your system to produce it.
