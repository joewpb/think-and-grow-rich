Chapter 07 · Decision · Worksheet

DECISION — COMMIT OR CUT

Principle: The Mastery of Procrastination · josie.ai Study Club
Hill's research across 25,000 failures found that lack of decision was near the top of the causes list. Not lack of talent. Not lack of resources. Lack of decision. The Declaration of Independence was signed by people who knew it might cost them their lives. Courage is not the absence of fear — it is the decision made in spite of it.
Fields — Complete Before or During Session
The 30-Day Pending Decision
Write the decision you've been deferring. State it precisely.
True Cost of Not Deciding
What does this non-decision cost you per week in energy, momentum, and opportunity?
The Information I Actually Need
List only the information genuinely required to make this decision. Separate this from the information you're using to delay it.
Commit or Cut — The Call
Make the decision right now. Write it here. Date it. Sign it.
Opinion Sources to Eliminate
Who are you currently consulting on this decision who has no stake in your outcome and should not be in the room?
Session Checklist
I identified the decision I've been deferring longest
I separated necessary information from delay-justifying information
I made and recorded at least one pending decision during this session
I named the opinion sources I'm removing from my process
This worksheet is yours. The answers that matter most are the ones that make you pause. If nothing made you pause, you answered too quickly.
