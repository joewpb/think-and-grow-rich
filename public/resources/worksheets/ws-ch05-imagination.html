IMAGINATION — IDEA DEVELOPMENT LOG

Principle: The Workshop of the Mind · josie.ai Study Club
Hill's Coca-Cola story is the data: a $500 formula — worth nothing to the doctor who owned it — became one of the largest businesses in the world because the buyer added one ingredient the doctor didn't know was missing: imagination applied to a plan. The formula was the raw material. The idea was the asset.
Fields — Complete Before or During Session
The Undervalued Asset
What do you currently have — knowledge, access, a relationship, a skill — that you haven't fully imagined the applications of? List without filtering.
The Raw Idea
State one raw idea that has been in your mind without a plan attached to it. This is your material.
Synthetic Build: What Exists That I Can Combine
Using what you know about your idea, list all existing elements (resources, people, systems, models) that could be recombined to give it form.
The Minimum Viable Plan
What is the smallest concrete plan you can build from the above that moves the idea from impulse to first physical step?
Creative Imagination Practice
Schedule 20 minutes this week with no input — no phone, no content. Write what emerges when you hold your definite want in mind in silence.
Session Checklist
I identified an undervalued asset I own
I built at least a skeleton plan from a raw idea
I have 20 minutes of creative silence scheduled this week
I understand the difference between synthetic and creative imagination as functional tools
This worksheet is yours. The answers that matter most are the ones that make you pause. If nothing made you pause, you answered too quickly.
