VOCABULARY: IMAGINATION

The Workshop of the Mind · josie.ai Study Club
Hill uses specific language with precision. Casual reading blurs these terms into general meaning. Operational mastery requires exact definitions.
Chapter Vocabulary — Five Core Terms
Synthetic Imagination
The imaginative mode that works with existing knowledge — recombining, reordering, repurposing what is already known into new configurations.
Creative Imagination
Direct access to knowledge not derived from personal experience — the source of original ideas, hunches, and inspiration. Develops through mastery of the other principles.
Infinite Intelligence
Hill's term for the universal source of knowledge accessible through the Creative Imagination when the mind is operating at high vibration.
Thought Impulse
The raw, formless beginning of an idea. Imagination gives it shape. Planning gives it structure. Action gives it physical existence.
The Formula
In the Coca-Cola story: the product whose value was multiplied by several orders of magnitude through the application of imagination to a plan. The metaphor for any undervalued asset in the reader's possession.
Usage Drill
Before Leaving This Page
Cover the definition column. For each term: state the definition aloud in your own words. If you can't do it without looking — you know what it means but you haven't installed the definition yet. Repeat until you can. Vocabulary precision is not pedantry — it determines whether you apply the principle correctly or approximate it.
Hill's language is exact. When he says "burning desire" he does not mean "strong preference." When he says "definiteness of purpose" he does not mean "clear goal." The imprecision with which most people read this book is the same imprecision with which they apply it.
