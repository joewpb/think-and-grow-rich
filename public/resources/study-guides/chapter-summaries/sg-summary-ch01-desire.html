Chapter 01 · Desire · Weeks 2–3 · Chapter Summary

DESIRE

The Starting Point of All Achievement · josie.ai Study Club
"Wishing will not bring riches. But desiring riches with a state of mind that becomes an obsession, then planning definite ways and means to acquire riches, and backing those plans with persistence which does not recognize failure, will bring riches."
Chapter Overview
Barnes arrived at Edison's office looking like a tramp — no capital, no credentials, no contacts. What he possessed was a desire so consuming it had eliminated the option of failure from his mental operating system. Hill structures this chapter around six concrete steps for converting desire into its physical equivalent, and uses the story of Hill's own deaf son as evidence that desire can override what appears to be a permanent limitation. The chapter's central argument: the difference between a wish and a desire is not emotional intensity — it is the elimination of alternatives. A person with a burning desire has no exit strategy.
Key Concepts at a Glance
The Six Steps
Hill's explicit protocol: fix an exact amount, determine what you give in return, set a definite date, create a definite plan, write it out, read it aloud twice daily. This is not metaphor — it is a literal instruction set.
Desire vs. Wish
A wish entertains the possibility of not getting what you want. A desire, as Hill defines it, has closed that possibility off. The distinction is not semantic — it determines the entire behavioral output that follows.
Burning the Ships
Barnes had no backup plan. Hill frames this not as recklessness but as the precise condition that made his outcome inevitable. The exit route, when left open, is the reason most people use it.
Adversity as Asset
Hill's son was born without ears. Hill refused the medical verdict and raised the child with the belief that his condition was a gift. The child developed hearing through a method his father couldn't have planned — and later spent years helping other deaf children. The limitation became the leverage.
The Money Consciousness
Hill says riches begin with a state of mind. Money consciousness must be deliberately cultivated — it does not develop by default. Its opposite — poverty consciousness — develops automatically without effort.
