INTRODUCTION

Principle: Thoughts Are Things · josie.ai Study Club
Hill opens with a warning: this book contains a secret. He won't hand it to you directly — he says if you're not ready to receive it, no amount of explicit telling will make it land. The intro is a test. It filters who's actually paying attention from who's just reading.
Discussion Questions · Session Protocol
Q01 · The Secret
Hill says the secret appears no fewer than 100 times in the book — but never stated plainly. What do you think it is, based on the introduction alone? Defend your answer.
↳ Come back to this question after Week 16. What changed?
Q02 · Edwin Barnes
Barnes arrived at Edison's office looking like a tramp, carrying nothing but a thought. What specific quality separated him from the thousands of others who wanted to work with Edison?
↳ Hill says it was not what Barnes had, but what he held in his mind.
Q03 · The Thousand Dollar Lesson
R.U. Darby quits drilling for gold three feet from a massive vein. Where in your current life are you potentially three feet from the breakthrough you've been building toward?
↳ This question is uncomfortable on purpose.
Q04 · Thoughts as Architecture
Hill states: 'Thoughts are things — and powerful things at that.' Most people treat thoughts as passive. How does treating your dominant thoughts as physical forces change what you allow yourself to think habitually?
Q05 · The Fifty-Cent Lesson
A child outmaneuvers a grown man through sheer refusal to accept 'no.' What does this tell us about persistence vs. strategy in getting what you want?
↳ Hill uses this story as proof that the principle works regardless of age, size, or circumstance.
Run the protocol: Pre-load → Signal → Friction → Capture. Every idea your own mind generates during this discussion belongs in your notes — not the book's ideas. Yours. That is the output Hill designed this club to produce.
