Chapter 01 · Desire · Discussion Guide

DESIRE

Principle: The Starting Point of All Achievement · josie.ai Study Club
Barnes didn't have credentials, capital, or connections. He had one thing: a burning, specific, non-negotiable desire that he refused to renegotiate with reality. Hill's argument is surgical — the gap between a wish and a desire is the gap between a person who talks about what they want and a person who builds their entire life around getting it.
Discussion Questions · Session Protocol
Q01 · Wish vs. Desire
Hill draws a sharp line between wishing for money and desiring it with obsession. Where exactly does that line fall — and which side of it are you on right now?
↳ Be precise. Vague answers reveal the answer.
Q02 · Burning the Ships
Barnes 'burned all bridges behind him' — no backup plan, no exit strategy. When has a forced commitment (no retreat) produced results you wouldn't have gotten otherwise? When has having an exit route cost you?
↳ Hill argues the backup plan is the reason people need one.
Q03 · The Six Steps
Hill gives six concrete steps for transmuting desire into money. Which of the six are you currently executing — and which are you skipping? What's the honest reason for each skip?
↳ Step 6 is the one most people skip. Why?
Q04 · Definiteness
Hill says 'fix in your mind the EXACT amount you desire.' Most people resist this with a vague 'I want more money.' What is your number — specific, with a deadline? If you don't have one, what does that tell you?
↳ The reader decides their own number. No prescribed figure.
Q05 · Adversity as Asset
Hill's son was born without ears. Hill turned the limitation into an advantage — the child believed his condition was a gift, and it became one. Where in your life is a perceived limitation actually a usable edge?
↳ This is the most transferable idea in the chapter.
