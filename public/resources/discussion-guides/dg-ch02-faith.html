Chapter 02 · Faith · Discussion Guide

FAITH

Principle: Visualization & Belief in Attainment · josie.ai Study Club
Hill strips the mysticism out of faith and delivers the engineering spec: any thought repeatedly passed to the subconscious — with feeling — eventually becomes belief, which the subconscious then treats as instruction. This works for confidence and it works for failure. Most people are running a faith program they installed by accident.
Discussion Questions · Session Protocol
Q01 · Faith as Engineering
Hill says 'Faith is a state of mind which may be induced.' This removes it from the realm of the miraculous and places it in the realm of habit design. What does it change for you to treat faith as a programmable mental state rather than something you either have or don't?
Q02 · The Criminologist Analogy
Hill compares building faith to how criminals are made — through repeated exposure until acceptance, then embrace. What beliefs about yourself did you 'acquire' through repeated environmental exposure that you never consciously chose?
↳ This is not a therapy question — it's an audit question.
Q03 · The Self-Confidence Formula
Hill includes a five-point formula to be memorized and repeated aloud daily. Most people will read it and move on. What is the realistic cost of actually following this instruction vs. not?
↳ Hill says it must be signed and repeated. He means it literally.
Q04 · Positive vs. Negative Faith
The same mechanism that builds belief in success builds belief in failure. Where in your life is your subconscious currently running a faith program pointed in the wrong direction — one you installed through repeated negative self-talk or environmental input?
Q05 · Deceiving the Subconscious
Hill suggests 'deceiving' the subconscious by acting as if you already have what you want. Where does this become delusional vs. where is it a legitimate engineering technique for belief installation?
↳ The line matters. Locate it precisely.
