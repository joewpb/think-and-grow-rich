THE SUBCONSCIOUS MIND

Principle: The Connecting Link · josie.ai Study Club
The subconscious never sleeps, never evaluates, and never takes a day off. It executes on whatever input it receives — whether you feed it deliberately or let the environment feed it by default. Seven major positive emotions. Seven major negative emotions. One will dominate. You choose, or the environment chooses for you.
Discussion Questions · Session Protocol
Q01 · The Filing Cabinet
Hill describes the subconscious as a field of consciousness that records every thought impulse and can be voluntarily used as a medium for transmutation. What are you currently filing — voluntarily and involuntarily — that will influence the output you receive?
Q02 · The Emotion Activation Requirement
Hill says the subconscious responds most readily to thoughts mixed with emotion. Cold logic alone doesn't penetrate it. What does this mean practically for how you structure your autosuggestion practice and your planning sessions?
↳ Emotion is the carrier, not the content.
Q03 · The 14 Emotions — Full Audit
Hill lists 7 positive emotions (desire, faith, love, sex, enthusiasm, romance, hope) and 7 negative emotions (fear, jealousy, hatred, revenge, greed, superstition, anger). Which specific emotions are currently most dominant in your mind? Positive or negative?
↳ Positive and negative cannot occupy the mind simultaneously. One dominates.
Q04 · Prayer as Technology
Hill reframes prayer as a transmission to Infinite Intelligence — but says it only works if approached with faith, not fear. Most people, he says, pray only after everything has failed, which means they pray in fear. What is the difference between praying/intending in fear vs. in faith — and which state are you in when you set your goals?
Q05 · The Subconscious as Garden
Hill uses the garden metaphor: if you don't plant deliberately, weeds grow. What is currently growing in your subconscious garden that you did not plant — and what specific planting practice would begin to displace it?
